# Self-Branding Domain — Shared Templates & Patterns

## Brand Brief Template
```markdown
## Personal Brand Brief: [Name]

### Identity
- Name: [Full name / professional name]
- Current role / title: [what they do today]
- Industry / domain: [field]
- Location: [city/country — relevant for speaking/media]
- Years of experience: [n]

### Positioning
- I want to be known as: [specific reputation goal]
- My target audience: [who I want to reach and influence]
- My unique angle: [what I know/have done that most people in my field haven't]
- My competition: [who else occupies this space — not to copy, to differentiate]

### Goals (prioritized)
1. [Goal 1 — e.g., "become a recognized voice in X for Y audience"]
2. [Goal 2 — e.g., "generate inbound consulting inquiries"]
3. [Goal 3 — e.g., "land speaking gigs at [conference type]"]

### Assets
- Current platforms: [list with follower counts]
- Published work: [articles, talks, posts — best examples]
- Credentials: [certifications, degrees, notable companies]
- Results / impact: [specific metrics from career — numbers]
- Network: [notable connections, communities]

### Voice
- Tone: [formal | conversational | provocative | empathetic | data-driven]
- What I never want to be seen as: [e.g., "corporate | salesy | arrogant"]
- Brands / people whose communication style I admire: [examples]
```

## Positioning Canvas (Personal Brand)
```markdown
## Personal Brand Positioning Canvas: [Name]

### Audience
Who exactly am I building this brand for?
[Specific: job title, career stage, industry, pain point]

### Problem I Solve for Them
What challenge do they have that my brand/content/expertise helps with?
[Specific: the actual frustration, not a category]

### My Unique Combination
What 2-3 things do I uniquely combine that most people in my space don't?
[Examples: "I combine deep technical knowledge with a talent for explaining it to executives"
"I have built 3 businesses AND have academic rigor in behavioral economics"]

### My Unfair Advantages
What do I have that most people in my space don't?
[Experience in unusual situations, access to rare data, specific accomplishments, unique network]

### My Point of View
What do I believe about my field that is different from the mainstream?
[The provocative take, the contrarian view, the thing I'd argue about at a dinner party]

### My Brand Promise
In one sentence: "[Name] helps [audience] [achieve outcome] by [mechanism/approach]"

### Proof Points (top 3)
1. [Specific result, accomplishment, or credential that backs the promise]
2. [Specific proof]
3. [Specific proof]
```

## Story Architecture Template
```markdown
## Personal Brand Story: [Name]

### The Origin
[Where did the journey start? The moment before the expertise existed.
What were you doing? What frustrated you? What didn't work?]

### The Turning Point
[What changed? The insight, the failure, the mentor, the experience
that set you on the path you're on now]

### The Build
[How did you develop this expertise? What did you do that most people don't?
What did you sacrifice, invest, or commit to?]

### The Proof
[What have you accomplished as a result?
Specific, measurable results — not vague "I help people with X"]

### The Mission
[Why does this work matter beyond just your career?
What change in the world does it contribute to?]

### The Invitation
[Why should someone follow you, work with you, or learn from you?
What do they get from being in your orbit?]
```

## LinkedIn Headline Formulas
```markdown
## LinkedIn Headline Formulas

### Formula 1: Role + Impact
[Job Title] who [specific result you deliver] | [secondary identity or credential]
Example: "VP Marketing who turns early-stage SaaS into category leaders | Angel investor"

### Formula 2: Transformation
Helping [audience] [achieve specific outcome] | [current role at company]
Example: "Helping founders go from $1M to $10M ARR | Growth at Acme"

### Formula 3: The Intersection
[Field 1] × [Field 2] = [unique outcome]
Example: "Engineering × Marketing = Products that sell themselves"

### Formula 4: Bold Claim + Proof
[Provocative claim] | [proof element] | [role]
Example: "Cold outreach gets 40%+ reply rates. Here's how. | Growth Lead at Acme"

### Formula 5: Audience-First
For [audience]: [specific value you provide] | [role]
Example: "For technical founders: the sales playbook you weren't taught | Founder at Acme"
```

## Case Study Template (Portfolio)
```markdown
## Case Study: [Project / Engagement Name]

### The Situation (before)
- Client/Context: [who they were + the situation]
- Challenge: [specific problem — with numbers if possible]
- What they'd already tried: [context on failed attempts]
- Stakes: [what was at risk]

### My Role
- What I was brought in to do: [specific scope]
- Unique contribution: [what I brought that others couldn't]
- Timeline: [duration]

### The Approach
[What I did — specific enough to be credible but not a how-to manual]
[Key decisions and why I made them]
[What I would do differently in hindsight — adds authenticity]

### The Results
| Metric | Before | After | % Change |
|--------|--------|-------|----------|
| [metric] | [n] | [n] | [%] |

### The Insight
[The one transferable lesson from this engagement]
[What this reveals about how to solve this type of problem]

### Testimonial (if available)
"[Quote from client — specific, result-focused]"
— [Name, Title, Company]
```
