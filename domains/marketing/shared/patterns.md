# Marketing Domain — Shared Templates & Patterns

## Campaign Brief Template
```markdown
## Campaign Brief: [Campaign Name]

### Objective
- Business goal: [increase signups | generate pipeline | launch product | retain customers]
- Campaign goal: [concrete measurable outcome]
- Success metric: [primary KPI + target number]

### Audience
- Primary segment: [who, job title, company size, pain point]
- Secondary segment: [if applicable]
- Exclusions: [existing customers, competitors, etc.]

### Messaging
- Core insight: [what does the audience believe/feel that we can leverage?]
- Main message: [one sentence that captures the campaign promise]
- Proof point: [stat, testimonial, or demo that backs it up]
- CTA: [specific action we want them to take]

### Channels
| Channel | Role (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU) | Budget | KPI |
|---------|----------------------|--------|-----|
| [channel] | [role] | $[n] | [metric] |

### Timeline
- Pre-launch: [assets, approvals, QA]
- Launch date:
- Duration:
- Key milestones:
- Debrief date:

### Budget
- Total: $[n]
- By channel: [breakdown]
- Production / creative: $[n]

### Creative Requirements
- Formats needed: [display sizes, video specs, copy lengths]
- Brand guidelines reference: [link]
- Messaging tone: [professional | conversational | urgent | inspiring]

### Measurement Plan
- Tracking setup: [UTM parameters, pixels, events]
- Reporting cadence: [daily | weekly]
- Optimization checkpoints: [day 7, day 14, day 30]
```

## Positioning Canvas
```markdown
## Positioning Canvas: [Brand / Product]

### Category
We are in the [category] market.

### Target Customer
[Job title] at [company type] who [key behavior/trigger].

### Problem We Solve
They struggle with [problem] because [root cause].

### Our Solution
We [solution verb] by [mechanism] so they can [outcome].

### Differentiation
Unlike [competitor approach], we [unique approach] because [proof].

### Proof
[Stat, customer story, or undeniable fact that backs up differentiation]

### Positioning Statement
For [target customer] who [have this problem],
[Brand] is the [category] that [key benefit]
unlike [alternatives], we [differentiator].
```

## Competitive Analysis Framework
```markdown
## Competitive Analysis: [Market / Category]

### Direct Competitors
| Competitor | Positioning | Price | Strengths | Weaknesses | Share |
|------------|-------------|-------|-----------|------------|-------|

### Indirect / Adjacent Competitors
[Who else solves the same problem differently?]

### Market Map (2x2)
Axes: [Axis 1 — e.g., Enterprise ↔ SMB] × [Axis 2 — e.g., Automation ↔ Manual]
[Plot each player. Identify white space.]

### Messaging Analysis
[What are competitors saying? What are they NOT saying?]

### Channel Analysis
[Where are competitors spending? Estimated budgets if available.]

### Opportunity
[Based on the above: where is the gap we can win?]
```

## A/B Test Hypothesis Template
```markdown
## A/B Test: [Test Name]

### Hypothesis
Because [observation / insight], we believe that changing [element]
from [control] to [variant] will [expected outcome]
as measured by [metric].

### Audience
- Segment: [who sees this test]
- Traffic split: [50/50 | 70/30 | etc.]
- Minimum sample size: [n per variant]

### Variants
- Control (A): [describe current state]
- Variant (B): [describe change]
- Variant (C): [if multi-variate]

### Success Criteria
- Primary metric: [metric] improvement of ≥ [%]
- Statistical significance: ≥ 95%
- Duration: [n days minimum]

### Risk
- What if variant loses? [rollback plan]
```
