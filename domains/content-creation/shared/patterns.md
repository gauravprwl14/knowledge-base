# Content Creation Domain — Shared Templates & Patterns

## Content Brief Template
```markdown
## Content Brief: [Title]

### Metadata
- Content type: [blog | guide | video script | podcast | thread | newsletter | case study]
- Platform: [where it will be published]
- Target length: [words | minutes | scenes]
- Author / Creator: [name]
- Due date: [date]

### Audience
- Who is this for: [persona or audience segment]
- What they already know: [assumed knowledge level]
- What they're trying to do: [JTBD — what they want to accomplish]
- What they fear / doubt: [objections or skepticisms to address]

### Goal
- Business goal: [brand awareness | lead generation | engagement | SEO | product education]
- Audience goal: [what they walk away knowing / able to do / feeling]
- CTA / next step: [what we want them to do after consuming]

### Content Strategy
- Angle / hook: [the specific point of view or frame that makes this different]
- Core argument (thesis): [the one idea this piece builds toward]
- Competing content: [what already exists on this topic — how do we beat it?]

### Research Requirements
- Data / stats needed: [specific evidence to gather]
- Experts to quote: [names or source types]
- Examples to include: [companies, stories, case studies]

### SEO (if applicable)
- Primary keyword: [keyword]
- Secondary keywords: [list]

### Outline (high-level)
1. [Opening — hook]
2. [Section 2]
3. [Section 3]
4. [Closing — CTA]

### Tone and Style
- Tone: [formal | conversational | provocative | empathetic | authoritative]
- POV: [first person | third person | second person]
- Reading level target: [grade level]
```

## Hook Formula Library
```markdown
## Content Hook Formulas

### Curiosity Gap
"I spent [X] years doing [thing] wrong. Here's what I wish I knew."
"Most [audience] don't know [surprising fact]. Here's why it matters."
"The one thing that [outcome] — and almost no one talks about it."

### Contrarian
"Everyone says [popular belief]. They're wrong."
"[Common advice] is bad advice. Here's why."
"Stop doing [common practice]. Do this instead."

### Specific Result
"How I went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]."
"[Company / person] achieved [specific result]. Here's exactly how."
"The [n]-step system that [specific outcome]."

### Relatable Problem
"If you've ever struggled with [problem], this is for you."
"Every [persona] faces this at some point. Most handle it wrong."
"[Problem] used to keep me up at night. Now it's solved."

### Fear / Warning
"[Common mistake] is killing your [metric]. Here's how to fix it."
"Before you [action], read this."
"The [n] signs you're about to [bad outcome]."

### Bold Claim
"[Provocative statement]. Here's my evidence."
"[Counterintuitive truth] — and the data backs it up."

### News Hook
"[Recent event] just changed everything about [topic]."
"In light of [news], here's what [audience] needs to know."
```

## Story Structure Templates

### The Problem-Solution Story
```
HOOK: [Vivid description of the problem moment]
CONTEXT: [Why this problem exists / why it's hard]
TURNING POINT: [What changed — the insight, the discovery, the decision]
SOLUTION: [What the solution is and how it works]
RESULT: [Specific outcome — with numbers if possible]
LESSON: [The universal truth / takeaway for the reader]
CTA: [What the reader should do with this information]
```

### The Transformation Narrative
```
BEFORE: [The character in their old world — clearly imperfect]
DESIRE: [What they want / their goal]
OBSTACLE: [What keeps getting in the way]
DISCOVERY: [The insight, tool, or approach they find]
TRANSFORMATION: [How they change — skills, mindset, results]
AFTER: [The new world they now live in]
INVITATION: [The reader can experience this too — here's how]
```

### The Contrarian Argument
```
PROVOCATIVE CLAIM: [State the bold take clearly]
CONVENTIONAL WISDOM: [Acknowledge what most people believe]
YOUR COUNTER-EVIDENCE: [Data, case studies, or logical argument]
THE REAL PRINCIPLE: [The underlying truth your contrarian view reveals]
IMPLICATIONS: [What this means practically for the reader]
CAVEAT: [Where the conventional wisdom does apply — credibility builder]
```
